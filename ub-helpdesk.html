Extra Credit Load

Information and requirements for this service

About This Service

Students are allocated 18 credits per semester as the standard load. If you need to take additional courses beyond this allocation, you must submit a formal request to the Registry for approval.

Students on academic probation are not eligible for a credit overload request.
Requirements
1
Minimum CGPA of 3.0
2
Valid academic reason (graduation requirements, course dependencies)
3
Supporting letter from your academic advisor (recommended)
How It Works
1
Complete the Extra Credits request form at the Registry
2
Provide a clear academic justification
3
Your faculty advisor may be contacted for verification
4
Registry will review and respond within 5–7 working days
Contact
Registry Services Office, Student Affairs Building, Main Campus, Gaborone
Email: registry@ub.bw
Office hours: Mon–Fri, 08:00–16:30

Add a Course

Information and requirements for this service

About This Service

You may request to add a course to your current semester registration. This is subject to seat availability, prerequisite completion, and timetable compatibility.

Requests submitted after Week 2 require Faculty Dean approval and may be subject to a late addition fee.
Requirements
1
Request submitted within the first 2 weeks of semester
2
Completion of any listed prerequisites
3
No timetable clashes with existing courses
How It Works
1
Visit the Registry and select the course you wish to add
2
Choose your preferred section and time slot
3
Provide a reason for the addition
4
Registry processes within 3–5 working days
Contact
Registry Services Office, Student Affairs Building, Main Campus, Gaborone
Email: registry@ub.bw
Office hours: Mon–Fri, 08:00–16:30

Drop a Course

Information and requirements for this service

About This Service

You may request to drop a registered course during the semester. The timing of your drop determines whether it appears on your academic transcript and whether financial penalties apply.

Dropping after Week 4 results in a "W" (Withdrawal) grade on your transcript. Dropping below 12 credits may affect your full-time status and financial aid.
Requirements
1
Valid reason for dropping (schedule conflict, medical, academic load)
2
Awareness of financial and academic implications
3
Supporting documentation for medical or compassionate drops
How It Works
1
Visit the Registry and select the course you wish to drop
2
Indicate the drop category and reason
3
Attach any supporting documents if applicable
4
Registry will confirm the withdrawal within 3 working days
Contact
Registry Services Office, Student Affairs Building, Main Campus, Gaborone
Email: registry@ub.bw
Office hours: Mon–Fri, 08:00–16:30

Change Programme of Study

Information and requirements for this service

About This Service

A programme change is a formal request to transfer from your current academic programme to a different one. This process involves faculty review, credit recognition, and administrative approval.

Credits completed in your current programme may be recognised by the receiving department at their discretion.
Requirements
1
Formal motivation letter
2
Approval from your current Faculty
3
Acceptance from the receiving Faculty
4
Good academic standing is recommended
How It Works
1
Select your target programme and preferred intake
2
Write a detailed motivation letter
3
Submit to the Registry with all supporting documents
4
Processing takes up to 15 working days after submission
Contact
Registry Services Office, Student Affairs Building, Main Campus, Gaborone
Email: registry@ub.bw
Office hours: Mon–Fri, 08:00–16:30

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common student queries

Student FAQs
Course Registration
How do I add a course to my registration?v
Navigate to "Add a Course" from the sidebar. Review the requirements and visit the Registry Services Office with your request. Additions are only permitted within the first two weeks of semester.
Can I drop a course after the semester has started?v
Yes. Drops within Weeks 1–2 leave no academic record. Drops in Weeks 3–4 may have financial implications. After Week 4, a "W" (Withdrawal) grade is recorded on your transcript.
What happens to my fees if I drop a course?v
If you drop within the first two weeks, you may be eligible for a partial refund. After that period, the full tuition for the course remains payable. Contact the Finance Office for your specific situation.
Credit Load
What is the standard credit allocation per semester?v
The standard allocation is 18 credits per semester. Taking more than 18 requires a formal extra credits request, subject to a minimum CGPA of 3.0 and Registry approval.
What CGPA do I need to apply for extra credits?v
A minimum Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) of 3.0 is required. Students on academic probation are not eligible.
Programme Changes
How long does a programme change take to process?v
Programme change requests take up to 15 working days to process after submission. This allows time for both your current and target faculty to review the application.
Will my completed credits transfer if I change programmes?v
Credits may be recognised by the receiving department at their discretion. A credit recognition assessment is conducted as part of the programme change review process.
General
How do I track the status of my request?v
You can track your request under "My Requests" in the sidebar. Requests are listed under Pending, Approved, or Denied with full details and any admin notes.
Who do I contact if my request is denied?v
If your request is denied, you have the right to appeal within 5 working days. Contact the Faculty Dean's Office with additional supporting documentation and a written motivation.
What are Registry Services office hours?v
The Registry Services Office is open Monday to Friday, 08:00–16:30. You can also email registry@ub.bw or call +267 355 0000.

Administrator FAQs

Reference guide for managing the UB Student Help Desk

Admin FAQs
Managing Requests
How do I approve or deny a student request?v
Navigate to "Pending Requests" from the sidebar. Each request card shows the student's details, reason, and request type. Use the Approve or Deny buttons to action each request. You can add a decision note that will be visible to the student.
Can I add a note when approving or denying a request?v
Yes. When actioning a pending request, there is a text field for an admin decision note. This note is displayed to the student on their request card and helps them understand the outcome or next steps.
How is the bell notification count calculated?v
The bell icon in the top bar shows the total number of requests currently in "Pending" status. This count updates each time a request is approved or denied.
Reports
What does the report page show?v
The Reports page shows a summary of all help desk activity: total requests, pending/approved/denied counts, a bar chart broken down by request type, a donut chart for status breakdown, and a full submissions table.
Can I print or export the report?v
Yes. Use the "Print Report" button at the top of the Reports page. This triggers a print-optimised view that hides navigation and formats the report cleanly for printing or saving as a PDF.
General
What request types can students submit?v
Students can submit four types of requests: Extra Credit Load (to exceed 18 credits), Add a Course, Drop a Course, and Change Programme of Study. Each type has its own eligibility criteria and processing timeline.
How do I contact a student about their request?v
Student contact details are managed through the main UB student information system. Use the student ID shown on each request card to look up contact details in the UB staff portal.
